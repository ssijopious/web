Research Topics in Generalized Association Rule Mining

Improving Measures

The objective is two overcome the two shortcomings of association rule mining. First, association rule mining will be generalized to numerical data and their aggregates (conditional expected values); second, adequate measures to practically cut down the complexity of multi-factor analysis reports will be developed.

Dimensional Unsupervised Learning

The objective is to develop more fine-grained concepts of dependence for the discrete-valued data dimensions of the given analytic scenario. Operationally, the objective is to design a reasonable pragmatics that exploits efficient clustering (unsupervised learning) against a notion of dependence thresholds.

Performant Implementation

The objective of this research is to provide the performant realization of the generalized association rule mining (as designed/elaborated in the companion PhD topic „Improving Measures“). Given the combinatorial explosion of multi-factor analysis in combination with the computation of the new, improved measures, the performant implementation is a challenge.
